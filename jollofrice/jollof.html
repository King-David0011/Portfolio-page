Jollof Rice Recipe

A classic Nigerian dish loved by all!

About Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a flavorful one-pot dish that is widely popular in West Africa, especially in Nigeria. It is made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and a mix of aromatic spices. Often served with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or fish, it is a centerpiece of celebrations and gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 6 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 4 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons of smoked paprika
  • 2 cups of chicken stock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Seasoning cubes (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot and saut√© the chopped onions until translucent.
  2. Add the blended tomatoes, bell peppers, and scotch bonnet peppers. Cook the mixture on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes until the oil begins to separate from the sauce.
  3. Stir in the thyme, curry powder, smoked paprika, and bay leaves. Cook for another 5 minutes to release the flavors.
  4. Add the chicken stock and season with salt and seasoning cubes. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Rinse the rice thoroughly to remove excess starch, then add it to the pot. Stir to combine.
  6. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with foil and a tight-fitting lid, and allow the rice to steam for 20-25 minutes.
  7. Check the rice occasionally and add a splash of water if it begins to stick. Once the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed, fluff with a fork.
  8. Serve hot with fried plantains, chicken, or fish.
